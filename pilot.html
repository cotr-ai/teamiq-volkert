How It Works

From kickoff to deliverables in 14 days

Every pilot follows a structured, repeatable process. Here's what happens after you sign on.
Days 1–2 Kickoff & Data Collection
We send you the SB and LB intake forms, collect your company profile, certifications, NAICS codes, past performance, and teaming interests. We also pull your active SAM.gov opportunity pipeline.
Days 3–5 Engine Ingestion & Scoring
Your data enters the TeamIQ scoring engine. Every SB + LB + Opportunity triad gets evaluated across all 8 weighted factors — set-aside eligibility, NAICS alignment, geographic proximity, past performance, partner capability, vehicle access, sole-source eligibility, and teaming readiness.
Days 6–9 Analysis & Gap Identification
We analyze the scored results, identify the highest-value teaming combinations, flag missing certifications or vehicles, and map sole-source pathways — including Tribal 8(a) opportunities with no dollar ceiling.
Days 10–12 Scout Brief Production
The Scout Brief is assembled — a professional, branded document with executive summary, scored opportunities, strength/gap analysis, sole-source callouts, and prioritized next steps. Ready to share with partners, BD leads, or executive sponsors.
Days 13–14 Delivery & Strategy Session
You receive the Scout Brief plus full match score exports. We walk through the findings in a 60-minute strategy call — covering top opportunities, recommended teaming pairings, and immediate actions to increase win probability.

Frequently Asked

Common questions

Everything we hear from new clients before they sign on.
What size company is the pilot designed for? ▾
TeamIQ works for both small businesses looking to partner with large primes, and large businesses seeking certified SB teaming partners. The pilot is designed for one client entity — either an SB or LB — and scores their full opportunity pipeline against potential partners.
What data do you need from us to get started? ▾
We need your completed intake form (provided at kickoff), which covers company profile, set-aside certifications, NAICS codes, geographic service areas, past performance summary, bonding capacity, and teaming history. The form takes about 20 minutes to complete and feeds directly into the scoring engine.
How is the composite score calculated? ▾
The composite score (0–100) is a weighted average of 8 factors: Set-Aside Eligibility (25%), NAICS Alignment (20%), Geographic Proximity (15%), Past Performance (15%), Partner Capability (10%), Vehicle Access (5%), Sole-Source Eligible (5%), and Teaming Readiness (5%). Each factor is scored independently, and the weights reflect real-world impact on win probability.
What happens after the pilot is complete? ▾
Most clients move to a recurring engagement — monthly SAM.gov pulls, continuous pipeline scoring, and updated Scout Briefs as new opportunities appear. The pilot is designed to demonstrate value quickly so you can make an informed decision about ongoing service.
Can I share the Scout Brief with partners? ▾
Absolutely. The Scout Brief is formatted as a professional, branded document specifically designed to be shared with teaming partners, BD leads, and executive decision-makers. It's your document — use it however serves your business development strategy.
